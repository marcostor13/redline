## Structural Mezzanine Installation — Illinois, Midwest & Nationwide

When you need more square footage but cannot expand your building footprint, a structural mezzanine is the most cost-effective solution available. Instead of relocating to a larger facility or investing in a building addition, a mezzanine platform creates a second level of usable space within your existing structure — typically at a fraction of the cost of new construction.

Redline Installers designs, permits, and installs structural mezzanine platforms for warehouses, distribution centers, and manufacturing facilities. Our crews have installed platforms ranging from 500 sq ft tool cribs to 40,000 sq ft multi-level storage systems.

### Types of Mezzanines We Install

**Free-standing structural steel mezzanines** are the most common and most versatile platform type. The structure is self-supporting, with columns that bolt to the floor independent of any racking system. This allows the platform to span large open areas and be configured for any combination of storage, office, or mechanical use. Free-standing platforms can also be disassembled and relocated when your operation changes facilities.

**Rack-supported mezzanines** are built directly on top of a compatible pallet rack system, using the rack uprights as the structural columns for the platform above. This is a cost-effective option when an existing rack system can support the additional load and you need overhead storage without a separate structural frame. We verify the existing rack's structural capacity before specifying a rack-supported design.

**Shelf-supported mezzanines** use industrial shelving as the structural base and are suited for lighter storage applications — typically pick modules, parts storage, or workstation areas where the live load per square foot is modest.

### What Mezzanines Can House

- Pallet storage with forklift or dock-leveler access
- Manual pick modules and order fulfillment workstations
- Office space over warehouse or production floor
- Equipment rooms, battery charging stations, and maintenance areas
- Conveyor integration for automated pick-and-pass operations

### Our Installation Process

1. **Site assessment** — we measure your available floor area and clear height, review column locations, assess floor slab condition, and document existing infrastructure (fire suppression, electrical, HVAC) that may affect platform height or placement
2. **Concept design** — we produce a preliminary layout showing the platform footprint, column grid, stair and gate locations, and edge protection; we refine this with your operations team before proceeding to engineering
3. **Structural engineering** — a licensed structural engineer produces stamped drawings for permit submission; drawings include column design, beam sizing, deck specification, and connection details
4. **Permit coordination** — we submit to the local building department, respond to plan review comments, and track approval status; we keep you informed throughout the process
5. **Fabrication and delivery** — structural steel components are fabricated to the engineered drawings; we coordinate delivery sequencing to minimize the on-site staging footprint
6. **Installation** — Redline crews assemble and bolt the structure, install decking, attach stair towers, and install safety gates and handrail systems to IBC requirements
7. **Final inspection and sign-off** — we schedule and attend the building department final inspection; you receive the certificate of occupancy and a complete permit package for your building records
