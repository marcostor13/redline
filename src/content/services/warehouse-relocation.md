## Professional Warehouse Relocation — Illinois, Midwest & Nationwide

Moving a warehouse is one of the most operationally complex projects a distribution or manufacturing company can undertake. A poorly managed relocation means weeks of lost productivity, damaged rack components, and a chaotic reinstallation that creates safety hazards. Redline Installers has managed facility relocations for over 34 years — from single-aisle moves within the same building to multi-facility consolidations spanning several states.

### What We Do

Our warehouse relocation service covers every phase of the move so you never have to coordinate between multiple vendors. We begin with a full inventory of your existing rack systems — capturing bay configurations, beam levels, column conditions, and anchor points. This documentation becomes the blueprint for the reinstallation.

We then manage:

- **Tear-down and labeling** — systematic disassembly with every component labeled by bay, row, and level for exact reconstruction
- **Component palletizing** — uprights, beams, and hardware packed to minimize transit damage
- **Freight coordination** — we work with flatbed and van carriers experienced in rack freight; we handle all load planning and scheduling
- **Destination site prep** — floor condition review, column layout verification, sprinkler clearance check before the first truck arrives
- **Reinstallation and anchoring** — full rebuild to manufacturer specifications with OSHA-compliant anchoring
- **Post-move inspection** — torque verification, level check, and a written safety report delivered at project close

### Our Relocation Process

1. **Discovery call and site walk** — we assess both origin and destination facilities, document rack inventory, and identify any components that should be replaced rather than relocated
2. **Project schedule** — a day-by-day timeline covering tear-down, transit, and reinstallation with clear milestones and your team's access windows
3. **Phased execution** — for active operations, we sequence work zones so you maintain throughput throughout the project
4. **Freight and logistics management** — we handle carrier booking, insurance certificates, and load planning; you receive tracking updates throughout transit
5. **Reinstallation to spec** — every bay rebuilt to the original layout or an improved configuration if your new footprint allows
6. **Final inspection and handoff** — written safety inspection report, torque log, and as-built drawings if required by your insurer or building department

### Industries We Serve

Warehouse relocations for 3PL operators, distribution centers, manufacturing facilities, food and cold storage operations, and industrial product distributors throughout Illinois and across the country.
