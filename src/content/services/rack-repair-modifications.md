## Pallet Rack Repair & Modifications — ANSI MH16.1 Compliant

Forklift impacts, overloading, and anchor failures are the three leading causes of rack damage in active warehouses. Left unaddressed, even minor column damage compounds into catastrophic failures that put workers at risk and expose facility operators to significant liability. Redline Installers provides targeted rack repairs that restore structural integrity without the cost and downtime of a full system replacement.

### Types of Damage We Repair

- **Upright column damage** — dents, bends, and tears from forklift impacts; we assess against ANSI MH16.1 thresholds and replace columns that exceed safe repair limits
- **Beam damage and deflection** — bent, cracked, or deflected beams are replaced; we verify the replacement beam matches the original load rating and profile
- **Anchor bolt failures** — loose or sheared anchors are a critical safety issue; we re-anchor to the manufacturer's specification and verify torque
- **Connection damage** — damaged beam-to-upright connectors and safety pins are replaced with manufacturer-spec components only; we do not use non-rated hardware
- **Frame straightening** — minor bowing that falls within ANSI repair tolerances can be addressed with approved column repair kits rated for the original load capacity
- **Row spacer and column protector installation** — proactive protection to reduce future forklift impact damage

### System Modifications We Handle

Beyond repair, we also reconfigure existing rack systems to match changing operational needs:

- **Beam level adjustments** — adding or raising beam levels to accommodate new product dimensions
- **Bay extensions** — adding bays to existing rows within the same structural system
- **Capacity upgrades** — converting selective rack to double-deep or adding wire deck for new product types
- **Aisle reconfiguration** — adjusting aisle widths for narrow-aisle or very-narrow-aisle forklift transitions
- **Rack relabeling** — re-posting capacity placards after any modification that affects rated load

### Our Repair Process

1. **Damage assessment** — a Redline inspector photographs and documents every area of concern against ANSI MH16.1 thresholds
2. **Written report** — you receive a prioritized repair list with repair-vs-replace recommendations and photographic evidence
3. **Scope approval** — we confirm the scope and schedule before any work begins; no surprise add-ons
4. **Controlled unloading** — we coordinate with your warehouse team to safely unload only the affected bays
5. **Component replacement** — manufacturer-spec parts only; we do not substitute non-rated hardware
6. **Anchoring and torque verification** — all anchors verified to specification with a calibrated torque wrench; results logged
7. **Inspection report** — a signed post-repair report documenting all work completed, parts used, and final load ratings
