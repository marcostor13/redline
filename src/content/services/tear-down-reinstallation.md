## Pallet Rack Tear-Down & Reinstallation — Illinois, Midwest & Nationwide

When a lease ends, a consolidation is planned, or a new facility becomes available, the rack system you have spent years configuring does not have to be abandoned. A professional tear-down and reinstallation project recovers that asset value and rebuilds your storage infrastructure at the new location — without starting over with new equipment.

Redline Installers has managed rack tear-down and reinstallation projects for single-aisle repairs to full facility moves exceeding 500,000 pallet positions. We treat every component with the care required to ensure it performs safely when it goes back up.

### The Value of a Professional Tear-Down

An improper tear-down damages components that would otherwise be reusable. Uprights dropped from height, beams thrown into unpadded piles, and connectors lost in the chaos of a rushed disassembly translate directly into replacement costs and installation delays at the destination. Redline crews work methodically — bay by bay, level by level — to protect the asset value of your rack system throughout the process.

### What We Salvage and What We Replace

Not every component survives a move in serviceable condition. Before any work begins, our crew lead completes a written condition assessment using ANSI MH16.1 damage criteria:

- **Uprights** — assessed for column dents, bends, and base plate damage; components that exceed repair thresholds are tagged for replacement before reinstallation
- **Beams** — checked for deflection, cracked welds, and damaged safety pins; bent or cracked beams are removed from inventory
- **Connectors and hardware** — safety clips, row spacers, and anchor hardware are inspected individually; worn or corroded hardware is replaced with manufacturer-spec components
- **Wire decking** — assessed for wire breaks, bowed frames, and waterfall damage; damaged decks are separated from serviceable units before palletizing

You receive a written salvage report with component counts so you can budget for replacements before the move begins.

### Logistics Coordination

Rack components are bulky, heavy, and require specific handling to avoid transit damage. We manage:

- **Palletizing and wrapping** — uprights are bundled by height and gauge, beams are stacked by length and profile, and all hardware is bagged and labeled by bay
- **Load planning** — we calculate freight requirements and specify proper loading sequence to protect components and distribute weight within DOT limits
- **Carrier coordination** — we work with flatbed and van carriers experienced in rack freight and obtain the certificates of insurance your landlords and risk managers require
- **Delivery sequencing at the destination** — we schedule freight arrivals to match the reinstallation crew's pace so the work area stays organized and safe

### Reinstallation

Reinstallation follows our standard installation process: site preparation, layout verification, OSHA-compliant assembly, anchoring to specification, and a final torque and level inspection. If the destination facility has different column spacing, ceiling height, or floor configuration than the origin, we adapt the layout to maximize the existing component inventory while maintaining the manufacturer's structural requirements.
