## Material Handling Systems — Illinois, Midwest & Nationwide

A warehouse is not a collection of independent equipment — it is a system. Pallet racks that are not sized to your forklift aisle, conveyor lines that do not align with your rack pick face, and mezzanine platforms that create traffic conflicts with your ground-level flow all reduce throughput and increase labor cost. Redline Installers approaches every project as a complete material handling system, installing each component with the others in mind.

We have designed and installed integrated material handling systems for operations from 10,000 sq ft regional distributors to 600,000 sq ft national fulfillment centers. The scale changes — the discipline of proper system integration does not.

### What We Install

**Pallet rack systems** form the storage backbone of most warehouse operations. We install selective, drive-in, push-back, pallet flow, and cantilever rack systems sized to your product mix, forklift type, and throughput requirements. Every rack system is anchored to specification and documented with bay-level load ratings.

**Conveyor infrastructure** connects storage to fulfillment. We install belt, roller, and sortation conveyor systems and align them precisely with rack pick faces, mezzanine decks, and ship-dock equipment. Conveyor installation is coordinated with your controls integrator to ensure mechanical interface points meet electrical and PLC requirements.

**Mezzanine platforms** create second and third levels of usable space within your existing building envelope. We install free-standing structural mezzanines, rack-supported platforms, and pick module systems that integrate directly with conveyor lines above and below.

**Mobile and ancillary storage** — picking carts, flow rack for pick-to-light modules, bulk storage shelving, drum storage systems, and work platform equipment — is positioned and anchored as part of the system layout rather than as an afterthought.

### Our System Design Process

1. **Operations assessment** — we document your product mix (SKU count, dimensions, weight range, turnover rate), throughput targets (orders per hour, pallets per day), equipment fleet (forklift types and aisle requirements), and staffing model (batch pick, zone pick, wave pick) before designing anything
2. **Layout development** — we produce a dimensioned floor plan showing rack layout, conveyor routing, mezzanine footprint, aisle widths, dock alignment, and forklift traffic patterns; we iterate with your operations team until the layout reflects how your business actually works
3. **Vendor coordination** — if you are purchasing equipment from multiple suppliers, we coordinate with each vendor's project manager to align delivery schedules, installation sequences, and interface specifications
4. **Sequenced installation** — structural elements first, then racking, then conveyors, then ancillary equipment; each phase is completed and verified before the next begins
5. **System commissioning** — we run product through the complete system at production speed before handoff, observe every interface point, and document all adjustments
6. **As-built documentation** — you receive dimensioned as-built drawings, component inventories, anchor torque logs, and rack load rating placards at project close
