## Conveyor System Installation — Illinois, Midwest & Nationwide

High-throughput distribution and fulfillment operations depend on conveyor systems to move product efficiently from receiving to storage to ship. A poorly installed conveyor line — misaligned rollers, undersized supports, improper belt tension — creates jams, product damage, and maintenance headaches from day one. Redline Installers provides professional conveyor system installation with the precision that high-volume operations demand.

Our crews install new conveyor systems, extend existing lines, and integrate conveyor infrastructure with mezzanine platforms, pallet rack systems, and sortation equipment.

### Conveyor Types We Install

**Belt conveyors** are the workhorses of order fulfillment — they transport cartons, polybags, and totes from induction through pick zones to pack and ship. We install horizontal runs, incline sections, and curve segments, with proper tensioning and drive alignment at every section.

**Powered roller conveyors** are used for accumulation zones, pack stations, and pallet transport. We install zero-pressure accumulation (ZPA) lines that prevent product damage in high-density queue zones, and standard powered roller lines for continuous-flow applications.

**Gravity roller and skate wheel conveyors** are low-cost, zero-energy solutions for short spans, packing stations, and truck loading dock lines. We size roller gauge and wheel spacing to your carton mix to ensure smooth product flow without tipping.

**Sortation systems** — including pop-up wheel sorters, sliding shoe sorters, and tilt tray systems — require precise mechanical alignment and careful coordination with controls integrators. We install the mechanical infrastructure to the millimeter tolerances that sortation equipment demands, then work alongside your controls team during commissioning.

**Chain and pallet conveyors** move heavy loads — pallets, drums, and bulk containers — through manufacturing and distribution operations. We install drag chain, roller chain, and towline systems for heavy-duty transport applications.

### Our Installation Process

1. **Pre-installation coordination** — we review the conveyor manufacturer's installation drawings, coordinate with the controls integrator on junction point locations, and walk the site to confirm floor levelness, column clearances, and overhead obstructions
2. **Structural supports and framing** — conveyor supports, floor anchors, and overhead hangers are installed first, leveled with precision, and verified against the layout drawings before any conveyor sections arrive
3. **Line assembly and alignment** — conveyor sections are assembled and aligned in sequence; we use laser levels and alignment tools to ensure roller centerlines are within tolerance across the full line length
4. **Drive and tensioning setup** — belt tension, drive chain slack, and take-up adjustments are set per manufacturer specification; we do not leave commissioning adjustments to the first week of operation
5. **Integration points** — scanner mounts, scale pads, label printer stands, and PLC junction boxes are installed to the controls integrator's specification so electrical work can proceed without rework
6. **Commissioning run** — we run product through all lines at production speed before handoff, observe for belt tracking issues, roller drag, and accumulation zone behavior, and document any adjustments made
7. **Operator walkthrough** — we walk your team through the system, point out adjustment locations, and provide a punch-list of any open items before the project is formally closed
