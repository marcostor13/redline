## Rack & Storage Installation for Manufacturing Facilities

Manufacturing facilities present installation challenges that pure warehouses do not: production equipment that cannot be moved, floor utilities and drainage that limit anchor placement, overhead crane systems that restrict rack height, and active production lines that cannot be stopped for facility work. Redline Installers has navigated these constraints for over 34 years — installing raw material storage, WIP rack systems, and finished goods infrastructure in manufacturing environments across Illinois and the Midwest.

### Storage Systems for Manufacturing Operations

**Cantilever rack** is the standard for long material storage in manufacturing facilities. Bar stock, structural steel, pipe, lumber, coiled wire, and fabricated components all require open arms that provide full-length access without vertical column obstructions. We size arm capacity, column spacing, and base bracing to the actual material weight and handling equipment — typically overhead crane, forklift with carpet pole attachment, or a floor-level material cart.

**Structural pallet rack** — bolted construction rather than roll-formed — handles the high point loads common in manufacturing environments where full pallet weights exceed standard selective rack specifications. We install double-slotted structural rack for operations storing heavy fabricated parts, castings, stamped components, and other dense materials that exceed the capacity limits of standard warehouse rack.

**Work-in-process storage** systems keep components organized and moving through production efficiently. We install flow rack for sequenced component presentation to assembly lines, bin shelving for small parts and fasteners, and tilted shelf systems for ergonomic component access at workstations.

**Finished goods rack** is designed to interface with your shipping dock and forklift fleet. We optimize aisle widths, dock alignment, and staging areas to minimize the travel distance from the last production step to the outbound trailer.

### Working in Manufacturing Environments

Manufacturing installations require a level of coordination that warehouse projects typically do not. We attend pre-construction meetings with your facilities team, production scheduler, and safety department. We obtain all required hot work permits and comply with your facility's safety program throughout the project. Our crews bring their own PPE, observe your lock-out/tag-out procedures in any area adjacent to active equipment, and submit daily work completion reports to your facilities manager.

Every anchor location is documented and reviewed against your floor utility drawings before drilling begins. Post-installation, you receive a complete inspection report with anchor locations, torque values, rack load ratings, and as-built dimensions for your facility records.
