## Food-Grade Rack Installation for Cold Storage & Food Distribution

Cold storage and food distribution facilities require rack installations that meet requirements standard warehouse systems do not: corrosion-resistant finishes that withstand refrigeration condensation cycles, clearances that allow sanitation equipment access, and anchor methods compatible with the vapor barriers and specialty concrete common in refrigerated facility construction.

Redline Installers installs pallet rack for dry grocery, refrigerated, and frozen food distribution environments throughout Illinois and the Midwest. We specify materials and finishes appropriate to your specific environment and design clearances that support your sanitation program.

### Rack Systems for Food Distribution

**Pallet flow rack** is the standard system for FIFO date rotation in perishable storage. Gravity-fed flow lanes ensure that the oldest product always exits first — essential for fresh produce, dairy, and any food product with a regulated use-by date. We size lane depth, roller pitch, and end-stop configuration to your pallet dimensions and typical load weight in both ambient and refrigerated configurations.

**High-density cold storage systems** maximize cubic utilization within expensive refrigerated space. Walk-in cooler and blast freezer square footage is significantly more costly to build and operate than ambient warehouse space — every wasted cubic foot represents ongoing refrigeration cost. We design drive-in and push-back systems that maximize density within your refrigerated envelope while maintaining the access your forklift fleet requires.

**Selective pallet rack** for ambient food warehousing and dry grocery distribution. Full SKU selectivity, adjustable beam heights for mixed product dimensions, and compatibility with counterbalanced, reach, and narrow-aisle forklifts. We design aisle widths and dock staging areas to support your receiving and outbound shipping velocity.

**Freezer and cooler rack with cold-rated components** — galvanized uprights and beams, cold-temperature-rated flow rollers, and stainless fasteners where required — specified for the actual operating temperature of your environment. We do not install ambient-rated components in refrigerated environments.

### USDA and FDA Facility Compliance

For USDA-inspected and FDA-regulated food facilities, rack installation documentation is part of your compliance record. We provide material specifications, finish certifications, clearance dimensions, anchor specifications, and load rating placards in a complete project file at installation close. When your facility undergoes regulatory inspection, you have the documentation to demonstrate that the rack system was installed to specification and is maintained to the appropriate standard.

Our crews follow your facility's GMP protocols during installation — no food contact, no unauthorized access to production or storage areas, and full adherence to your visitor and contractor hygiene requirements.
