## Distribution Center Installation — Built for Throughput

Distribution centers live and die on throughput. Every misaligned rack bay, improperly tensioned conveyor, or poorly designed pick aisle costs labor hours and reduces on-time ship performance. Redline Installers builds distribution center infrastructure with throughput as the primary design constraint — every system element positioned to support your pick, pack, and ship workflow.

### What We Install for Distribution Centers

**Selective and push-back pallet rack** forms the bulk storage backbone for most DC operations. We install selective systems optimized for your reach truck or counterbalanced forklift, with aisle widths engineered to your specific equipment turning radius. Push-back and pallet flow lanes are integrated where velocity warrants higher density without sacrificing selectivity across the full facility.

**Conveyor systems** connect storage to fulfillment. We install belt, powered roller, and sortation conveyor lines — aligned precisely with rack pick faces, mezzanine induction points, and ship-dock equipment. Every installation is coordinated with your controls integrator to ensure mechanical interface points meet PLC and electrical requirements before controls work begins.

**Mezzanine pick modules** create multi-level order fulfillment space within your existing building envelope. We install structural steel platforms with integrated conveyor lines, pick-to-light infrastructure mounts, and forklift-accessible pallet drop zones — designed as a complete pick module system rather than isolated components.

**Sortation system mechanical infrastructure** for high-volume e-commerce and omnichannel DC operations. Pop-up wheel sorters, sliding shoe sorters, and divert lane mechanical frames installed to the millimeter tolerances that sortation equipment demands.

### Our Approach to DC Installations

Distribution center installations are complex coordination challenges — multiple equipment vendors, controls integrators, WMS implementations, and operational deadlines all converging at once. Redline manages the mechanical installation as a single coordinated project, not a series of independent tasks.

Our crew leads understand DC operations — zone picking, batch picking, wave management, dock scheduling — and install systems that support your workflow rather than fighting it. We attend pre-construction coordination meetings with your project team, document every interface specification, and flag conflicts between vendors before they become field problems.
