## Warehouse Rack Installation for 3PL & General Warehousing

General warehousing operations demand storage systems that maximize cubic utilization while remaining flexible enough to accommodate variation in product size, turnover rate, and handling requirements across a changing client base. Redline Installers has installed rack systems for third-party logistics providers, public warehouses, and single-tenant distribution operations throughout Illinois and the Midwest for over 34 years.

### What We Install for Warehousing Operations

- **Selective pallet racking** — full front access, adjustable beam heights, compatible with any standard counterbalanced or reach truck; the standard for high-SKU 3PL operations
- **Drive-in and push-back racking** — high-density storage for high-volume, low-SKU inventory; push-back provides LIFO access for 2–6-deep lanes without a dedicated forklift aisle
- **Pallet flow racking** — gravity-fed FIFO for date-sensitive product and perishables; ideal for fast-moving SKUs in high-throughput warehouses
- **Mezzanine platforms** — adds a second level of storage or pick space above the warehouse floor without expanding the building footprint
- **Wire decking and rack accessories** — waterfall decks, row spacers, column protectors, and aisle signs that complete a professional, OSHA-compliant installation

### How We Design for Warehousing

Every warehouse has a different mix of forklift equipment, dock locations, column spacing, and fire suppression zones. We do not apply a generic template. Our process starts with a full site assessment that documents your floor dimensions, column grid, ceiling height, sprinkler clearances, and dock locations. We then design a dimensioned rack layout optimized for your specific forklift model and aisle width requirements.

For 3PL operators with mixed client inventories, we identify which product segments benefit from high-density storage and which require full selectivity — then design a hybrid system that maximizes utilization across the full facility without sacrificing operational flexibility.
